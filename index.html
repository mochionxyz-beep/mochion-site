Proof over promises
the bet Almost every trading operation is a black box. You hand money to people you can't see and hope the numbers they show you are real. Mochion is built the other way round — don't trust, watch. Every move the machine makes is out in the open, wins and losses, printed as it happens; belief you can check for yourself instead of a pitch you have to swallow. It's a small machine with a big idea: if trading this open can earn your trust, maybe the black boxes should have to open up too.
✦ ✦ ✦

What is Mochion?

A little machine that trades crypto — carefully. One person built it, it runs on its own on Bybit and Hyperliquid, and every move it makes gets written down. No hype, no promises. Just a machine, doing its job in the open.

Soft on the outside. Strict on the inside.

Meet the Cast

four little jobs · one little machine

No.1
The Referee
the safety switch

Watches every strategy like a referee. The moment one stops working, it blows the whistle and benches it — before it can lose much.

No.2
The Dispatcher
the front desk

Runs the front desk. Takes each order, double-checks it, sends it to the exchange, and makes sure what really happened matches the plan.

No.3
The Lookout
the scout

Keeps watch for safe, boring opportunities. When it spots one it waves a flag — but never trades alone. A human always presses the button.

No.4
The Archivist
the study

Studies the past all day to learn what actually worked. The careful one that keeps everyone else honest.

✦ ✦ ✦

When it breaks, you'll hear about it

Most trading pages only show the wins. This one owns up to the mistakes too — the bugs, the things that broke, the ideas that stopped working. Honest is more interesting than perfect.

The mess-ups get written up in the build log.

Not losing is the whole point

Mochion is built to be careful, not lucky. It keeps its bets small, drops what isn't working fast, and double-checks its own moves. The goal is to survive the bad weather — not to promise sunshine.

✦ ✦ ✦
The Tape
the proof This is where the talk ends and the receipts begin. Soon it'll show how the machine is really doing — the ups, the downs, all of it — straight from the system, every hour. An honest record, not a highlight reel. Early on there won't be much to see, and past results never promise future ones.
